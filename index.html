1. Cross-Task Overview

This experiment tests whether latent action pretraining generalizes across LIBERO tasks. We evaluate four pretraining data sources (Ego-10K 66hr, Ego-10K 200hr, Company 66hr, and EgoDex 66hr) at multiple pretraining durations (10K, 25K, and 50K steps) on three downstream tasks, with Baselines (no pretraining) for each.

Note on baselines: Ego-10K, 200hr, and Company experiments share one baseline (trained on the same instance). EgoDex was run on a separate instance and has its own independent baseline runs. EgoDex gaps are computed against the EgoDex baseline; all others against the shared baseline.

11. Key Findings & Analysis

1. Pretraining consistently helps on drawer and stove, but not butter

This is the clearest finding: all 18 non-EgoDex pretrained conditions beat baseline on both drawer and stove. EgoDex helps on drawer (+4.8pp) and stove (+3.3pp at 50K), but catastrophically hurts butter (−30pp at 50K). Only 2/8 non-EgoDex pretrained conditions beat baseline on butter.

2. 3× more data (200hr vs 66hr) does NOT improve performance

Average improvement: +0.3pp across all tasks and step counts. Score: 3 wins, 3 losses, 2 ties. The bottleneck is not pretraining data quantity at the 66–200hr scale.

3. Data source quality ranking

Ranking of pretraining sources (by average pretrained model SR across all tasks):

  1. Company 66hr — best on drawer, strong on stove
  2. Ego-10K 200hr — matches Company overall despite 3× data
  3. Ego-10K 66hr — close to 200hr, best on butter at 10K
  4. EgoDex 66hr — worst across the board, destructive on butter

4. EgoDex is the worst pretraining source

Despite being egocentric manipulation video (seemingly relevant), EgoDex underperforms all other data sources on every task. EgoDex loses 6/6 vs Ego-10K and 6/6 vs Company. The Apple dexterous manipulation data may be too domain-specific or too different from LIBERO's manipulation style.

7. EgoDex butter anomaly

The EgoDex baseline achieves 51.2% on butter — 10pp above the shared baseline (41.1%) — yet EgoDex pretrained models score only 21–33%. This suggests EgoDex pretraining actively overwrites useful features for fine-grained pick-and-place, and the effect worsens with more pretraining steps.

8. Diminishing returns implication

The 66hr → 200hr result strongly suggests that at PaliGemma-3B scale with latent action pretraining, data diversity matters more than data quantity. Both Ego-10K datasets are egocentric video of people manipulating objects — adding 3× more of the same type of data doesn't unlock new capabilities. EgoDex data, despite being "different" (dexterous manipulation), is too far from the target domain and actively hurts. Future scaling should focus on domain-matched data diversity or model scale rather than more hours of any single source.

9. High per-seed variance remains a challenge

EgoDex 25K stove ranges from 48.8% to 77.6% (28.8pp spread). Butter seed variance is extreme across all sources. These variance levels make it difficult to draw strong conclusions from 3-seed experiments, particularly on harder tasks.
